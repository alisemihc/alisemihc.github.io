About

On the machinations that shape perception, the enclosure of knowledge, and the search for what lies beyond the material.

Who I Am

I am a writer and researcher interested in the intersection of philosophy, technology, and governance. My work draws from continental philosophy, particularly Heidegger's concept of Gestell, alongside political economy, knowledge commons theory, and traditional metaphysics.

I believe that the most urgent questions of our time are not merely technical but ontological: how do the systems we build reshape what we can know, who we can become, and what we are permitted to see? These are the questions I pursue.

Areas of Focus

Philosophy of Technology

Heidegger's Gestell, the question concerning technology, and how modern frameworks shape and constrain human perception.

Knowledge Commons

Ostrom's governance frameworks applied to knowledge, information asymmetries, and the politics of enclosure.

AI Governance

The political and philosophical dimensions of artificial intelligence: who controls it, who benefits, and what it forecloses.

Tradition and Metaphysics

The immaterial, the transcendent, and the suppression of non-material knowledge by modern epistemic frameworks.
